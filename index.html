About the project

The Arcadia Lucky 9 Heritage Hunt is an Eagle Scout Service Project designed to make Arcadia's rich history accessible and engaging for residents, visitors, families, and students of all ages.

Participants visit 9 historic landmarks across the city, listen to narrated audio guides, solve riddles tied to each location's real history, and collect clues that lead them to the next stop — all without needing a tour guide.

The hunt exists in two formats: this digital app, and a printed booklet available at the Gilb Museum for those who prefer a non-digital experience or want a keepsake souvenir.

The Gilb Museum of Arcadia Heritage has preserved and celebrated the city's story since 2001. This project was created in partnership with the museum to expand their educational outreach and bring Arcadia's history to life in a new, interactive format.

About the creator

This project was designed and built by Shaunak Sathe as an Eagle Scout Service Project for Boy Scouts of America Troop 2131. It represents the culmination of planning, community partnership, historical research, app development, and volunteer coordination.

How to use the app

Start at the Gilb Museum, then follow the clues in order. The app uses GPS to confirm you're near each stop before unlocking the riddle. Solve each riddle, submit your answer, and a clue to the next location appears automatically.

All 9 stops are concentrated along Huntington Drive and the adjacent Arboretum area. Zone A stops are all walkable from the Gilb Museum. Zone B (the Arboretum cluster) requires a short drive west on Huntington Drive.

Accessibility: This app works on any phone browser — no download required. The printed booklet version is available free at the Gilb Museum for visitors who prefer a non-digital experience. The route is largely flat and follows public sidewalks.
